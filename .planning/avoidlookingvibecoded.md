# How To Avoid Looking Like You Were Coded By An AI

> If your design feels like it could have come from an AI prompt result, it has failed.

> Design should feel inevitable, not generated.

This guide outlines essential UI/UX improvements to elevate your software from a "vibe-coded" project to a professional product. Use this reference when making UI/design decisions, **before** asking the AI to build.

## 1. Refine Visuals for Professionalism

### Ditch the Emojis
While some applications can integrate emojis effectively, for most professional software, they instantly signal an unpolished, AI-generated aesthetic.

**Replace them with:** Sleek, professional icons (Phosphor Icons, Lucide, Heroicons)
- Emojis feel playful and casual
- Icons feel intentional and designed
- **Exception:** Consumer social products can sometimes use emojis, but even there, use sparingly

### Master Color Theory
AI often defaults to bright, clashing colors that detract from user experience.

**Instead, integrate color strategically:**
- Use data visualization elements (micro charts, graphs) to add visual interest with purpose
- Consider software color theory to create harmonious and purposeful schemes
- Use **color as information**, not decoration
- Each color should have meaning (status, hierarchy, interaction state)
- Default to neutral grays with one accent color
- Reserve bright colors for CTAs, alerts, or semantic meaning

### Color Strategy
- **60-30-10 rule:** 60% neutral, 30% secondary, 10% accent
- **Accessibility first:** Ensure WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum)
- **Dark mode:** Should be inversion, not a complete redesign
- **Semantic colors:** Success (green), Warning (yellow/orange), Error (red) — but muted, not neon

## 2. Optimize Layouts for Intuitive Navigation

### Information Architecture
AI frequently produces redundant or inefficient layouts.

**Focus on:**
- **Logical flow** — organize screens to follow user mental models
- **Consolidated navigation** — combine related items, remove redundant nav elements
- **Clear hierarchy** — use size, position, and contrast to guide the eye
- **Tighten spacing** — remove unnecessary gaps, but maintain breathing room
- **Progressive disclosure** — show advanced options only when needed

### User Profiles
**Avoid:** Generic, AI-generated gradient profile circles

**Use instead:** More sophisticated account cards that can house:
- Avatar
- Name
- Role/organization
- Account settings link
- Usage stats or relevant info

### Settings and Actions
**Avoid:** Scattered settings across multiple screens

**Use:**
- Organized popovers or modals for secondary options
- Collapse advanced options by default
- Group related settings logically
- Provide search for settings when many options exist

### Cards and Complexity
**AI tendency:** Over-complicate cards with too much information

**Fix:**
- Collapse actions into triple-dot menus on individual cards
- Organize information clearly within cards
- Remove redundant or decorative elements
- Use visual hierarchy to guide attention

## 3. Develop Intelligent Features and Modals

### Strategic Use of Modals
**When designing forms or input fields:**
- Opt for modals for sparse information
- Flyouts can work for complex, multi-step processes
- Modals provide focused interaction that guides users through specific tasks

### Form UX
**By default, collapse advanced options within forms to prevent overwhelming users**

**Ensure forms include:**
- All necessary options (AI often misses context-specific fields)
- Ability to swap between options (e.g., custom domains vs provided)
- Clear validation and error messages
- Proper input types and masks

---

## 4. Humanize Billing and Usage Pages

### Eliminate Redundancy
- Remove cards or elements that lack functionality or provide no real value
- If it doesn't help users understand or do something, remove it

### Structure for Clarity
- Retitle sections to be descriptive
- Use tabs to organize content effectively
- Remove generic KPIs that don't provide actionable insights

### Visualizing Data
- Replace generic KPIs with meaningful two-column layouts
- Add visual elements like small doughnut charts for data density
- Make data scannable and interpretable

### Transparency in Pricing
- Simplify pricing plans — reduce the number of options
- Clarify hierarchy between plans
- **Highlight discounts users are receiving**
- Clearly outline what's included in the next tier to encourage upgrades
- Ensure pricing information is accessible and well-integrated

---

## 5. Elevate Analytics for Richer Insights

### Focus on Actionable Data
- Discard generic KPIs and tabs that don't provide deep insights
- Add toggles that allow users to compare items side-by-side

### Enhanced Data Presentation
- Integrate more useful information and icons into rows
- Move beyond basic bar charts
- Consider: maps with shaded regions for geographic data
- Provide richer, more intuitive visualizations

## 6. Craft Compelling Landing Pages

### Build Trust Through Quality
Your landing page is often the first interaction a potential customer has with your software.

**Critical for:**
- Establishing trust
- Driving conversions
- Setting expectations

**AI-generated landing pages often lack:**
- Polish
- Professional graphics
- Clear presentation
- Compelling copy

### What Works
- **Professional-grade graphics:** Stylized link cards, visual effects, edited screenshots
- **Clear presentation:** Over complexity
- **Social proof:** Testimonials, case studies, user counts
- **Strong value proposition:** What you do, for whom, why different
- **Quality design signals:** Attention to detail in spacing, typography, colors

## 7. The Art of Emotional Design

### Creating Addictive Apps
Products that feel good to use build strong connections and drive growth.

**Case Study 1: Duolingo — Delight and Habit Formation**
- **Human-like character animation** — full facial reactions, lip-syncing
- **Emotional feedback loops** — animations respond to user actions
- **Celebrates small wins** — encourages continued engagement
- **Mascots with expressions** — characters feel alive

**Case Study 2: Phantom — Building Trust in Intimidating Spaces**
- **Animation as brand** — made crypto feel less scary
- **Polish as trust** — every micro-interaction signals quality
- **Approachable design** — friendly visuals for complex topics
- **Design for everyone** — not just early adopters

**Case Study 3: Revolut — Selling Premium Through Design**
- **Elevated first impressions** — onboarding feels premium
- **Tactile interactions** — dragging charts feels physical and responsive
- **Dynamic 3D cards** — cards flip, rotate, catch light
- **Quality is intentionality** — smooth feedback increases confidence

### Emotional Design Principles
**For habit-forming products (check-ins, journaling, daily use):**
- Instant emotional feedback (bounces, glows, sparkles on confirmation)
- Celebrate small wins with intentional success states
- Mascots use expressions to encourage users

**For high-stakes products (finance, health, insurance):**
- Polish builds trust — visual details, motion, transitions
- Approachable visuals — friendly, warm details for heavy topics
- Design for everyone — intuitive flows for everyday people
- Smooth performance and feedback — confidence in correctness

## 8. Common Anti-Patterns (AI Defaults to Avoid)

### Over-Gradients
- **Avoid:** Gradient backgrounds on everything
- **Use:** Subtle gradients sparingly for depth or emphasis
- **Better:** Solid colors with subtle shadows

### Generic Hero Sections
- **Avoid:** Large undefined hero images with vague copy
- **Use:** Specific, grounded visuals that communicate your actual product
- **Better:** Data-focused hero showing actual use cases

### Excessive Whitespace
- **Avoid:** So much whitespace that content feels lost
- **Use:** Intentional spacing that guides the eye
- **Better:** Tighter layouts with clear information density

### Cookie-Cutter Components
- **Avoid:** Same 3 cards, same layout as everyone else
- **Use:** Layouts that reflect your specific product's needs
- **Better:** Custom layouts optimized for your use case

### Generic Illustrations
- **Avoid:** 3D abstract shapes, people sitting at computers
- **Use:** Screenshots of your actual product, data visualizations
- **Better:** Real content from your application

## 9. The Core Principle

### Quality is Intentionality
Every element should feel like it was placed deliberately by a designer who understood:

- **Why this element exists** — what problem it solves
- **Why it looks this way** — how it serves the user
- **Why it's here, not elsewhere** — strategic placement
- **Why it uses these colors/shapes** — semantic meaning

### The "Generated" Feel Comes From:
1. **Lack of constraint** — AI tries to do everything
2. **Lack of context** — AI doesn't know your specific users
3. **Lack of iteration** — first attempt is rarely best
4. **Lack of editing** — AI outputs without refinement

### How to Counter:
1. **Edit aggressively** — remove everything non-essential
2. **Add constraints** — work within specific brand guidelines
3. **Know your users** — design for specific people, not "everyone"
4. **Iterate based on real usage** — test and refine based on behavior
5. **Make opinionated choices** — strong point of view beats generic appeal

## Quick Checklist Before Shipping

- [ ] No emojis in professional interface
- [ ] Colors are semantic, not decorative
- [ ] Each color has contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1
- [ ] No generic gradients without purpose
- [ ] Hero section shows actual product, not abstract shapes
- [ ] User profiles feel intentional, not generic
- [ ] Settings are organized, not scattered
- [ ] Cards have clear information hierarchy
- [ ] Forms collapse advanced options
- [ ] KPIs are actionable, not vanity metrics
- [ ] Pricing highlights discounts and next-tier value
- [ ] Analytics support comparison and detail, not just summary
- [ ) Landing page has professional graphics, not generated ones
- [ ] Micro-interactions feel responsive and intentional
- [ ] Overall feeling: "This was designed by humans who cared"

---

**Remember:** If your design feels like it could have been generated by a prompt, iterate. Add constraint. Add specificity. Add opinion. Add soul.
