Extract the most important claims from the following research paper.

Focus on:
- Primary hypotheses and research questions
- Key findings and contributions (novel results)
- Methodological claims (approach effectiveness)
- Statistical results with significance levels
- Conclusions well-supported by evidence
- Claims about causal relationships or correlations
- Comparative claims (X outperforms Y)

For each claim, provide:
1. claim_text: The exact claim statement (1-2 sentences, clear and specific)
2. claim_type: One of: assertion, fact, finding, hypothesis, method
   - assertion: A confident statement or proposition
   - fact: A verifiable statement or established result
   - finding: A research result or discovery
   - hypothesis: A proposed explanation or prediction
   - method: A claim about methodology effectiveness
3. confidence: How confident you are this is a core claim (0.5-1.0, be selective)
4. section: Which section this claim comes from (Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion)
5. quote: A brief quote or paraphrase from the paper supporting this claim
6. page_number: Approximate page number if evident from the text

Quality guidelines:
- Extract only significant, non-trivial claims that contribute to the paper's main contributions
- Avoid generic statements like "we conducted a study" or "more research is needed"
- Prefer specific, testable claims with clear supporting evidence
- Include statistical significance when available (p-values, confidence intervals)
- Capture comparative and superlative claims
